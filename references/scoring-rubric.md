# Idea Scoring Rubric

Each idea is scored across 5 dimensions, each on a 1–5 scale.
The goal is honest calibration, not inflated optimism. A "3" is a perfectly viable starting point.

---

## 1. 実現可能性 (Feasibility)

Can this be built with reasonable effort and available technology?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Fully achievable with existing tools/APIs, no research required |
| 4 | Achievable with some integration work or learning curve |
| 3 | Achievable but requires specialized knowledge (ML, medical data, etc.) |
| 2 | Major technical hurdles — custom models, hardware, regulatory approval |
| 1 | Currently not technically feasible or requires breakthrough research |

**Factors to consider**: data availability, API access, AI model capability, regulatory constraints
(e.g., medical claims require FDA compliance), hardware requirements (e.g., spectrometer for
skin analysis)

---

## 2. 開発期間 (Development Time — Solo/Small Team)

How long would an MVP take for a solo developer or 2-person team?

| Score | Meaning | Timeframe |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| 5 | Very fast — can ship MVP in under 4 weeks | < 1 month |
| 4 | Fast — MVP in 1–2 months | 1–2 months |
| 3 | Medium — MVP in 2–4 months | 2–4 months |
| 2 | Slow — MVP in 4–6 months | 4–6 months |
| 1 | Very slow — requires 6+ months to reach anything shippable | 6+ months |

**Calibration**: Assume a capable solo full-stack developer with standard cloud tools.
Content-heavy products (databases, editorial) take longer than tool-based products.

---

## 3. 収益性 (Revenue Potential)

Can this generate meaningful revenue within 12–18 months of launch?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Clear monetization with high willingness-to-pay (beauty consumers spend $50–200/month) |
| 4 | Good monetization pathway with proven comparable products |
| 3 | Viable but requires scale (ads, affiliate) or premium tier to work |
| 2 | Difficult monetization — users expect free, or market is too small |
| 1 | Very hard to monetize — unclear business model |

**Beauty industry note**: Skincare/makeup consumers have high ARPU compared to average apps.
Subscription ($9–29/month), affiliate/referral commissions, and brand partnerships are the
three reliable models. Avoid ad-only models unless you expect very high DAU.

---

## 4. 競合優位性 (Competitive Advantage / Blue Ocean)

How crowded is this space? Is there a defensible angle?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Clear gap — no direct competitors, or all competitors are failing this niche |
| 4 | Some competition but obvious differentiation angle (underserved demographic, new tech) |
| 3 | Competitive space but beatable with better UX or niche focus |
| 2 | Crowded — well-funded direct competitors already dominating |
| 1 | Red ocean — large incumbents (L'Oreal, Sephora, Amazon) already own this |

**Key insight**: Niche demographics (mature skin, textured hair, sensitive skin, male grooming)
and niche use cases (post-chemo hair, pregnancy-safe formulas, perimenopause skin) are often
underserved despite large user bases.

---

## 5. 小規模開発適性 (Small Team / Solo Suitability)

How well is this idea suited to being built and run by a small team (1–3 people)?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Perfect indie/solo project — can launch, grow, and sustain with 1 person |
| 4 | Manageable with 1–2 people, minimal ongoing operations burden |
| 3 | Doable but will feel stretched — needs some automation or part-time help |
| 2 | Requires dedicated team — content, partnerships, support can't be solo'd |
| 1 | Fundamentally unsuited to small teams — logistics, regulatory, or supply chain complexity |

**Factors that hurt small team score**: physical product, medical claims, large content
editorial requirements, real-time customer support expectations, complex supply chain.

**Factors that help**: SaaS/digital-only, self-serve, community-driven content, automation-friendly.

---

## Composite Score Interpretation

| Total (out of 25) | Interpretation |
|-------------------|---------------|
| 22–25 | Exceptional — prioritize immediately |
| 18–21 | Strong — worth prototyping |
| 13–17 | Viable — proceed with awareness of weak dimensions |
| 8–12 | Challenging — needs a key insight to unlock |
| 5–7 | Not recommended — fundamental barriers |
